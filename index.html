About

Fifteen years rebuilding the way work gets done underneath businesses that grew faster than their ops could keep up.

The bottleneck is almost always the same shape: the business works, but only one person knows how to make it work. Usually the founder. Sometimes the expert who started it.

My job is to get it out — map the calls you make, write down the method, build the systems that let other people run the work without losing what made it good in the first place. AI is the best tool we have for this and we will use it, but that isn't the point.

I live in Pittsburgh with four daughters — a systems challenge in itself.
